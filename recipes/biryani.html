Biryani

Description

Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish that originated in the Indian subcontinent and has since become popular worldwide.

Biryani varies regionally, with each version offering its own unique twist. Hyderabad, Kolkata, Lucknow, and Malabar are some famous styles, each distinguished by ingredients and preparation techniques. Often served with cooling raita or tangy salan, biryani is a festive dish that brings people together for celebrations, family meals, and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 2 Cups Basmati Rice
  • 500 Kg Chicken
  • 1 Cup Yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • 2 large Onions
  • 2 Tomatoes
  • ¼ cup chopped coriander leaves
  • ¼ cup chopped mint leaves
  • 2-3 tablespoons cooking oil or ghee
  • A few strands of saffron soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk (optional)
  • Fried onions

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken:In a bowl, mix chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice. Cover and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (longer for better flavor).
  2. Cook the rice:Boil water with bay leaf, cloves, cardamoms, cinnamon, and salt. Add washed basmati rice and cook until it's 70–80% done (grains should still be firm). Drain and set aside.
  3. Fry the onions:In a deep pan or biryani pot, heat oil or ghee. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Remove half for garnishing later.
  4. Cook the chicken:In the same pan, add chopped tomatoes to the remaining onions. Cook until soft. Add the marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is tender and oil starts to separate.
  5. Layer the biryani:Lower the heat. Spread half the cooked rice over the chicken. Sprinkle some mint, coriander, fried onions, and saffron milk. Repeat with the remaining rice and garnish again.
  6. Dum (Steam cook):Cover the pan tightly with a lid (seal edges with dough if needed). Cook on very low flame for 15–20 minutes. Alternatively, place the pot over a tawa (griddle) to avoid direct heat.
  7. Serve:Gently mix before serving. Enjoy hot with raita, salad, or boiled eggs!
