Idly

Description

Idly is a popular South Indian breakfast dish made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram lentils).

Its simplicity, ease of digestion, and versatility make it a favorite not just in the South, but across the country and even among international food lovers.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups parboiled rice (idly rice)
  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram, skinned)
  • 1 tsp fenugreek seeds (optional, for better fermentation)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed for soaking and grinding)
  • A few drops of oil (for greasing idly molds)

Steps

  1. Soaking: Rinse 1 cup of urad dal (black gram) and 2 cups of parboiled rice separately. You can also add 1 teaspoon of fenugreek seeds to the urad dal while soaking. Soak both the dal and rice in enough water for about 4 to 6 hours.
  2. Grinding: After soaking, drain the water. First, grind the urad dal to a smooth and fluffy batter, adding water gradually. Then grind the rice to a slightly coarse texture. Mix both batters together in a large bowl. Add salt to taste and stir well to combine. The batter should be thick but pourable.
  3. Fermentation: Cover the bowl and keep it in a warm place for 8 to 12 hours, or overnight. During this time, the batter will ferment and rise. You should see small bubbles and the volume will increase, indicating it's ready to use.
  4. Steaming: Grease the idly molds with a few drops of oil. Pour the fermented batter into each mold, filling them about ¾ full. Steam them in an idly steamer or in a pressure cooker without the whistle for about 10 to 12 minutes on medium heat.
  5. Serving: After steaming, allow the idlies to cool for a couple of minutes. Then, gently remove them using a spoon. Serve hot with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, or sambar for a delicious and nutritious meal.

Enjoy your soft, fluffy idlies fresh and warm — they’re little pillows of happiness on a plate!
